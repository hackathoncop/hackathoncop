# Hackathon Community of Practice

A community for professionals who use hackathons as deliberate practice in the AI era.

---

## Why This Exists

AI has lowered the barrier to execution.

You can prototype faster.
You can automate more.
You can ship sooner.

The temptation is to outsource thinking.

The risk is drifting out of the loop.

Hackathons provide something rare:
a constrained, time-bound arena where you must build, decide, synthesize, and understand under pressure.

They are not just events.
They are deliberate practice.

Hackathon CoP exists for professionals who treat hackathons as a repeatable learning loop — not as competitions, not as résumé items, but as a way to stay sharp.

---

## What We Mean by “Community of Practice”

A community of practice is simple:

People who get better by practicing together.

There is no curriculum.
No hierarchy.
No central organizer.

Members:

* Enter hackathons regularly
* Form teams
* Share what they build
* Reflect on lessons learned
* Stay engaged with the craft

Practice comes first.
Structure follows only when needed.

---

## Who This Is For

* Engineers, builders, product thinkers, designers
* Professionals already operating in the field
* People who want to experiment without waiting for permission
* Builders who prefer the arena over abstraction

Students are welcome.
Curiosity is required.
Agency is expected.

---

## How To Participate

1. Join the organization.
2. Use Discussions to:

   * Signal interest in upcoming hackathons
   * Form teams
   * Share show-and-tell
   * Reflect on outcomes

Keep it practical.
Keep it respectful.
Keep it builder-focused.

---

## The Spirit

In the age of agents, staying in the loop matters.

Practice compounds.

Enter the arena.
